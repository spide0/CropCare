Introduction to Post-Harvest Transport Systems

The post-harvest transport system is a critical phase in the agricultural supply chain. It involves the movement of harvested produce from farms to markets, processing centers, or storage facilities. Proper transportation helps reduce spoilage and maintains the quality of perishable goods such as fruits, vegetables, and dairy products.

This document provides an extensive analysis of the various aspects of post-harvest transportation, including infrastructure, challenges, innovations, and strategies to minimize losses while maximizing profits.

Key Components of Post-Harvest Transport

Post-harvest transportation encompasses several key elements:

  • Transportation Modes: Trucks, railways, air cargo, and maritime shipping.
  • Cold Chain Logistics: Ensuring temperature-controlled transport for perishable goods.
  • Packaging: Protective packaging to prevent mechanical damage.
